## 1. Time Series Overview

**File**: `outputs/plots/mtbm_time_series_overview.png`

### What It Shows
A 6x4 grid showing 23+ parameters over time, grouped by function:
- Row 1: Survey Position (tunnel length, deviations)
- Row 2: Survey Orientation (yaw, pitch, reel, temperature)
- Row 3: Steering Control (4 cylinder strokes)
- Row 4: Operational (advance speed, forces)
- Row 5: Cutter Wheel (pressure, RPM, earth pressure)
- Row 6: Efficiency Metrics

### How to Read It

#### Color Coding by Group
- **Orange** = Survey Position parameters
- **Green** = Survey Orientation parameters
- **Red** = Steering Control parameters
- **Purple** = Operational parameters
- **Brown** = Cutter Wheel parameters
- **Purple** = Efficiency metrics

### Good Indicators ‚úÖ

1. **Smooth Trends**
   - Parameters should change gradually
   - No sudden spikes or drops
   - Example: Tunnel length should increase steadily

2. **Stable Deviations**
   - Horizontal and vertical deviations oscillate around zero
   - Total deviation stays low (<50mm)
   - Corrections are smooth, not abrupt

3. **Coordinated Cylinder Movement**
   - All 4 cylinders show related patterns
   - Move together to correct deviations
   - Stay within 20-80mm stroke range

4. **Consistent Operational Parameters**
   - Advance speed maintains steady pace
   - Working pressure stays in 140-180 bar range
   - RPM relatively constant (7-11)

### Warning Signs ‚ö†Ô∏è

1. **Erratic Behavior**
   - Sharp vertical lines (parameter jumping suddenly)
   - Indicates possible sensor malfunction or ground condition change
   - **Action**: Investigate cause, check sensors

2. **Trending Away from Normal**
   - Continuous increase/decrease beyond normal range
   - Example: Temperature steadily rising above 30¬∞C
   - **Action**: Schedule maintenance, check cooling systems

3. **Cylinder Saturation**
   - One or more cylinders stuck at extreme positions (0mm or 100mm)
   - Cannot provide further correction
   - **Action**: Adjust tunnel alignment strategy

4. **Declining Efficiency**
   - Drilling efficiency trending downward
   - More pressure needed for same advance speed
   - **Action**: Check cutter wear, inspect chamber

## 2. Deviation Analysis

**File**: `outputs/plots/mtbm_deviation_analysis.png`

### Panel 1: Machine Deviation Pattern (Top Left)

**What It Shows**: Scatter plot of horizontal vs vertical deviation with tolerance circles

**How to Read**:
- **X-axis**: Horizontal deviation (mm)
- **Y-axis**: Vertical deviation (mm)
- **Color gradient**: Represents tunnel progress (purple = start, yellow = later)
- **Circles**:
  - Green (¬±25mm) = Excellent tolerance
  - Orange (¬±50mm) = Acceptable tolerance
  - Red (¬±75mm) = Poor tolerance

**Good Outcomes** ‚úÖ:
- Points clustered near origin (0, 0)
- Most points within green circle
- Random scatter (no systematic bias)
- Color progression shows learning (early purple points may be farther out, later yellow points closer to center)

**Concerning Patterns** ‚ö†Ô∏è:
- Points systematically on one side (e.g., all positive horizontal)
  - **Meaning**: Systematic steering bias
  - **Action**: Recalibrate steering system

- Points forming a line
  - **Meaning**: One type of deviation causing the other
  - **Action**: Check for misaligned survey equipment

- Points outside red circle
  - **Meaning**: Severe misalignment
  - **Action**: STOP - reassess tunnel alignment, possible resurvey needed

- Color gradient moving outward (purple near center, yellow far out)
  - **Meaning**: Deteriorating performance over time
  - **Action**: Check for equipment wear or changing ground conditions

### Panel 2: Deviation vs Tunnel Progress (Top Right)

**What It Shows**: Total deviation as tunnel advances

**How to Read**:
- **X-axis**: Tunnel length (meters)
- **Y-axis**: Total deviation (mm)
- **Lines**: Quality thresholds (green at 25mm, orange at 50mm, red at 75mm)

**Good Outcomes** ‚úÖ:
- Line stays mostly below green (25mm) threshold
- Smooth oscillations around 10-30mm
- Deviations quickly corrected (spikes return to baseline)
- Stable or decreasing trend over time (operator learning)

**Concerning Patterns** ‚ö†Ô∏è:
- Consistently above orange line (50mm)
  - **Severity**: Medium - requires attention
  - **Action**: Increase steering corrections, review alignment strategy

- Any excursions above red line (75mm)
  - **Severity**: High - critical issue
  - **Action**: Pause operations, conduct survey, implement corrective measures

- Increasing trend (deviation growing with tunnel length)
  - **Severity**: High - accumulating error
  - **Action**: Check survey equipment calibration, verify reference points

- High-frequency oscillations (rapid up-and-down)
  - **Severity**: Medium - overcorrection
  - **Action**: Reduce steering sensitivity, smooth out corrections

### Panel 3: Steering Cylinder Activity (Bottom Left)

**What It Shows**: The 4 hydraulic cylinder strokes over time

**How to Read**:
- **X-axis**: Time
- **Y-axis**: Cylinder stroke position (mm)
- **4 colored lines**: One for each cylinder (Cyl 1, 2, 3, 4)

**Cylinder Layout** (typically):
```
      Top
   Cyl 2 (T)
       |
Left - + - Right
Cyl 3  |  Cyl 1
       |
   Cyl 4 (B)
    Bottom
```

**Good Outcomes** ‚úÖ:
- Coordinated movement (when Cyl 1 extends, Cyl 3 retracts)
- All cylinders stay in 30-70mm range (mid-stroke)
- Smooth changes, not jerky
- Opposite cylinders mirror each other

**Concerning Patterns** ‚ö†Ô∏è:
- One cylinder pinned at 0mm or 100mm
  - **Meaning**: Cylinder can't extend/retract further
  - **Action**: Change steering strategy, may need to accept current deviation temporarily

- All cylinders moving same direction
  - **Meaning**: Not steering, just shifting machine position
  - **Action**: Verify alignment target, check cylinder control system

- Rapid oscillations
  - **Meaning**: Control system instability or operator overcorrection
  - **Action**: Adjust control parameters, review steering protocol

- Cylinders not responding (flat lines during deviation)
  - **Meaning**: Control system failure or hydraulic issue
  - **Action**: Check hydraulic pressure, verify control signals

### Panel 4: Steering Force vs Deviation (Bottom Right)

**What It Shows**: Relationship between steering effort and deviation amount

**How to Read**:
- **X-axis**: Total steering force (kN)
- **Y-axis**: Total deviation (mm)
- **Color**: Advance speed (purple = slow, yellow = fast)

**Good Outcomes** ‚úÖ:
- Low force, low deviation (bottom-left region)
- Color distribution balanced (not all one color)
- Weak correlation (scattered points)
  - **Meaning**: Steering is effective and responsive

**Concerning Patterns** ‚ö†Ô∏è:
- High force, high deviation (top-right region)
  - **Meaning**: Steering hard but not effective
  - **Possible causes**: Difficult ground, worn equipment, misaligned machine
  - **Action**: Assess ground conditions, check equipment

- High force, low deviation (bottom-right)
  - **Meaning**: Over-steering
  - **Action**: Reduce steering sensitivity

- All yellow points (high speed) at high deviation
  - **Meaning**: Going too fast to maintain accuracy
  - **Action**: Reduce advance speed in critical alignment sections

## 3. Performance Dashboard

**File**: `outputs/plots/mtbm_performance_dashboard.png`

### Panel 1: Speed vs Pressure Performance (Top Left)

**What It Shows**: How advance speed relates to working pressure

**How to Read**:
- **X-axis**: Working pressure (bar)
- **Y-axis**: Advance speed (mm/min)
- **Color**: Revolution speed (RPM) - purple = low, yellow = high

**Good Outcomes** ‚úÖ:
- Points form upward-sloping cloud (higher pressure ‚Üí higher speed)
- Color gradient follows same pattern (yellow points at top-right)
- Consistent relationship throughout project
- Points clustered around regression line

**Optimal Operating Point**:
- Pressure: 150-170 bar
- Speed: 25-35 mm/min
- RPM: 8-10 (greenish color)

**Concerning Patterns** ‚ö†Ô∏è:
- High pressure, low speed (bottom-right region)
  - **Meaning**: Difficult ground conditions or equipment problems
  - **Causes**: Hard rock, clay adhesion, worn cutters
  - **Action**: Assess geology, inspect cutting tools

- Low pressure, low speed (bottom-left region)
  - **Meaning**: Inefficient operation
  - **Action**: Increase operating parameters if safe

- Scattered points (no clear relationship)
  - **Meaning**: Inconsistent operations or variable conditions
  - **Action**: Review operating procedures, check ground conditions

### Panel 2: Drilling Efficiency Trend (Top Middle)

**What It Shows**: How efficiently the machine converts pressure to progress

**How to Read**:
- **X-axis**: Tunnel length (m)
- **Y-axis**: Drilling efficiency (mm/min per bar of pressure)
- **Formula**: Advance Speed √∑ Working Pressure

**Good Outcomes** ‚úÖ:
- Stable trend around 0.15-0.25
- Slight increase over time (operator learning)
- Smooth line without major jumps

**Concerning Patterns** ‚ö†Ô∏è:
- Declining trend
  - **Meaning**: Taking more pressure to achieve same speed
  - **Causes**: Cutter wear, changing geology, increased friction
  - **Action**: Schedule inspection, may need cutter replacement

- Sudden drops
  - **Meaning**: Encountered difficult conditions or equipment issue
  - **Action**: Investigate location, correlate with geological survey

- Increasing trend to >0.3
  - **Meaning**: Unexpectedly easy conditions
  - **Consider**: May indicate voids or unstable ground (proceed cautiously)

### Panel 3: Pressure Balance Analysis (Top Right)

**What It Shows**: Earth pressure vs working pressure relationship

**How to Read**:
- **X-axis**: Working pressure (bar)
- **Y-axis**: Earth pressure (bar)
- **Red dashed line**: 1:1 ratio (perfect balance)

**Concept**: Earth pressure should roughly match working pressure to maintain stable excavation chamber.

**Good Outcomes** ‚úÖ:
- Points clustered near red 1:1 line
- Slight scatter acceptable (¬±20%)
- No systematic bias above or below line

**Concerning Patterns** ‚ö†Ô∏è:
- Points consistently above 1:1 line
  - **Meaning**: Earth pressure > Working pressure
  - **Risk**: Material could flow back into chamber
  - **Action**: Increase working pressure or reduce advance rate

- Points consistently below 1:1 line
  - **Meaning**: Working pressure > Earth pressure
  - **Risk**: Over-excavation, ground subsidence
  - **Action**: Reduce working pressure, check for voids

- Wide scatter
  - **Meaning**: Unstable chamber conditions
  - **Action**: Review spoil removal rate, check chamber sensors

### Panel 4: Interjack Force Distribution (Bottom Left)

**What It Shows**: Histogram of propulsion force usage

**How to Read**:
- **X-axis**: Interjack force (kN)
- **Y-axis**: Frequency (count of occurrences)
- **Red dashed line**: Mean force

**Good Outcomes** ‚úÖ:
- Bell-shaped distribution (normal distribution)
- Mean around 1000-1200 kN
- Most values between 800-1400 kN
- Symmetric shape

**Concerning Patterns** ‚ö†Ô∏è:
- Bi-modal distribution (two peaks)
  - **Meaning**: Operating in two distinct modes
  - **Possible cause**: Different ground types, operational shifts
  - **Action**: Investigate operational differences

- Skewed right (long tail toward high forces)
  - **Meaning**: Frequently encountering high-resistance conditions
  - **Action**: May need increased capacity or modified approach

- Mean above 1400 kN
  - **Meaning**: High resistance overall
  - **Concerns**: Equipment stress, accelerated wear
  - **Action**: Review geology, check equipment condition

- Very wide distribution (flat histogram)
  - **Meaning**: Highly variable conditions
  - **Action**: Expect variable progress rates, plan accordingly

### Panel 5: Temperature Monitoring (Bottom Middle)

**What It Shows**: Survey equipment temperature over time

**How to Read**:
- **X-axis**: Time
- **Y-axis**: Temperature (¬∞C)
- **Red line**: High temperature warning (30¬∞C)
- **Blue line**: Low temperature warning (15¬∞C)

**Good Outcomes** ‚úÖ:
- Stable around 20-25¬∞C
- Slight variations throughout day acceptable
- Stays between warning lines

**Concerning Patterns** ‚ö†Ô∏è:
- Approaching or exceeding 30¬∞C
  - **Risk**: Equipment malfunction, measurement errors
  - **Action**: Check cooling, verify sensor readings, pause if exceeds 35¬∞C

- Below 15¬∞C
  - **Risk**: Condensation, sensor accuracy issues
  - **Action**: Check environmental sealing

- Increasing trend
  - **Risk**: Developing thermal issue
  - **Action**: Monitor closely, schedule maintenance

- Sudden jumps
  - **Risk**: Sensor malfunction
  - **Action**: Verify with backup measurements

### Panel 6: Survey Mode Usage (Bottom Right)

**What It Shows**: Pie chart of survey system usage

**Modes**:
- **ELS (Electro-optical Laser System)**: Standard precision guidance
- **ELS-HWL (High Water Level)**: Used when water present
- **GNS (Gyroscopic Navigation System)**: Backup/alternative method

**Good Outcomes** ‚úÖ:
- Primarily using ELS (60-80%)
- Minimal GNS usage (<10%)
- Mode changes correlate with known conditions

**Concerning Patterns** ‚ö†Ô∏è:
- High GNS usage (>20%)
  - **Meaning**: ELS having difficulty
  - **Possible causes**: Alignment issues, equipment problems
  - **Action**: Investigate ELS performance

- Frequent ELS-HWL usage without known water conditions
  - **Meaning**: Possible water infiltration
  - **Action**: Verify waterproofing, check for leaks

## 4. Correlation Matrix

**File**: `outputs/plots/mtbm_correlation_matrix.png`

### What It Shows
Heat map showing how every parameter relates to every other parameter.

### How to Read

**Correlation Values**:
- **+1.0** (Dark red): Perfect positive correlation (both increase together)
- **0.0** (White): No correlation (independent)
- **-1.0** (Dark blue): Perfect negative correlation (one increases, other decreases)

**Colors**:
- **Red shades**: Positive correlation
- **Blue shades**: Negative correlation
- **White**: No relationship

### Expected Strong Correlations ‚úÖ

These are normal and indicate system working correctly:

1. **Tunnel Length ‚Üî Timestamp** (+0.9 to +1.0)
   - Should be strongly positive (more time = more tunnel)

2. **Horizontal Deviation ‚Üî Horizontal Drill Head Deviation** (+0.7 to +0.9)
   - Machine and drill head move together

3. **Working Pressure ‚Üî Earth Pressure** (+0.5 to +0.7)
   - Should maintain balance

4. **Advance Speed ‚Üî Revolution RPM** (+0.3 to +0.5)
   - Higher RPM generally allows higher speed

5. **Cylinder 01 ‚Üî Cylinder 03** (-0.5 to -0.7)
   - Opposite cylinders work against each other (negative correlation normal)

6. **Total Force ‚Üî Cylinder Strokes** (+0.4 to +0.6)
   - Cylinder extension creates force

### Concerning Correlations ‚ö†Ô∏è

These indicate problems:

1. **Any Parameter ‚Üî Itself ‚â† 1.0**
   - **Meaning**: Data error or sensor failure
   - **Action**: Check data quality

2. **Advance Speed ‚Üî Working Pressure** (<0.2)
   - **Meaning**: Pressure not translating to progress
   - **Problem**: Equipment inefficiency or difficult ground
   - **Action**: Investigate cause

3. **Earth Pressure ‚Üî Working Pressure** (<0.3 or >0.9)
   - **Too low**: Unstable chamber conditions
   - **Too high**: Sensors measuring same thing (sensor problem)
   - **Action**: Verify chamber operations, check sensors

4. **Unexpected Strong Correlations** (|r| > 0.8 between unrelated parameters)
   - Example: Temperature ‚Üî Horizontal Deviation
   - **Meaning**: Likely spurious correlation or sensor cross-talk
   - **Action**: Investigate measurement systems

5. **Deviation Parameters ‚Üî Steering Cylinders** (<0.1)
   - **Meaning**: Steering not responding to deviations
   - **Problem**: Control system failure
   - **Action**: Check steering control system immediately

### Using Correlation Matrix for Troubleshooting

**Scenario 1**: Advance speed decreasing but no change in pressure
- **Check**: Correlation between speed ‚Üî pressure
- **If low**: Pressure not being effective (worn cutters likely)
- **Action**: Schedule cutter inspection

**Scenario 2**: High deviations despite active steering
- **Check**: Correlation between deviation ‚Üî cylinder strokes
- **If low**: Steering not responding properly
- **Action**: Check hydraulic system and control logic

**Scenario 3**: Erratic readings on multiple parameters
- **Check**: Multiple unexpected strong correlations
- **If present**: Likely sensor/data logging issue
- **Action**: Verify data acquisition system

## 5. Decision Making Guide

### Immediate Stop Conditions (RED) üõë

Stop operations immediately if you observe:

1. **Total deviation > 100mm**
2. **Temperature > 35¬∞C**
3. **Any cylinder stuck at 0mm or 100mm for >1 hour**
4. **Earth pressure > 30 bar**
5. **Multiple parameters showing erratic behavior**
6. **Correlation matrix showing unexpected strong correlations (sensor failure)**

**Actions**:
- Halt boring
- Conduct equipment inspection
- Verify survey measurements
- Consult engineering team

### Caution Conditions (YELLOW) ‚ö†Ô∏è

Proceed with caution and increased monitoring:

1. **Total deviation 50-75mm**
2. **Temperature 30-35¬∞C**
3. **Drilling efficiency declining by >20%**
4. **Earth pressure 25-30 bar**
5. **Advance speed <15 mm/min consistently**

**Actions**:
- Increase monitoring frequency
- Prepare corrective measures
- Schedule maintenance during next break
- Document conditions for engineering review

| Temperature | 20-25¬∞C |
| Drilling Efficiency | 0.18-0.25 mm/min/bar |

## Conclusion

These visualizations provide a comprehensive view of MTBM operations. By regularly reviewing them and understanding the patterns:

1. **Prevent problems** before they become critical
2. **Optimize performance** by identifying best operating conditions
3. **Maintain quality** through continuous deviation monitoring
4. **Extend equipment life** by catching wear indicators early
5. **Improve efficiency** by learning from operational data

Remember: **Trends are more important than individual points.** Always look for patterns over time rather than reacting to single measurements.
